Ghanaian Banku and Tilapia

Ingredients:

For the Banku:

  • 2 cups fermented corn dough
  • 1 cup cassava dough
  • Water as needed
  • Salt to taste

For the Tilapia:

  • 2 whole tilapia, cleaned and scaled
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon chili pepper
  • 1 teaspoon anise (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon juice from 1 lemon
  • Oil for frying

For the Pepper Sauce:

  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1 onion
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 hot pepper (Scotch bonnet or habanero)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

For the Banku:

  1. Mix the fermented corn dough with water to a thick, smooth paste.
  2. Add the cassava dough and mix thoroughly.
  3. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly to prevent lumps, until the mixture thickens and cooks through.
  4. Add salt to taste and continue cooking until the banku becomes firm and elastic.
  5. Shape the banku into serving-sized balls and set aside.

For the Tilapia:

  1. Marinate the tilapia with garlic, ginger, chili pepper, anise, salt, and lemon juice for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry the tilapia until golden brown and cooked through.
  3. Alternatively, grill the tilapia for a healthier option.

For the Pepper Sauce:

  1. Blend the tomatoes, onion, garlic, and hot pepper with a little water to a smooth consistency.
  2. Pour the mixture into a saucepan, add salt to taste, and simmer until it thickens.

To Serve:

  1. Place a ball of banku on each plate, with a piece of tilapia alongside.
  2. Serve with the pepper sauce and additional raw vegetables if desired.
